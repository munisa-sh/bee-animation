01
about bee

Bees can actually recognize and remember human faces, not just other bees. But one bee in its entire lifetime produces only about 1/12 of a teaspoon of honey. So all that sweet honey? It’s the result of insane teamwork by millions of bees. Without them, we’d be in serious trouble—because they pollinate around 70% of all the crops we eat. Bottom line: bees keep the world running, not the other way around.

02
Honeybee

Honeybees aren’t just cute insects making honey. They have a crazy social system with strict roles — workers, drones, and one queen ruling the hive. The queen can lay up to 2,000 eggs per day. Worker bees communicate by dancing to tell others where to find food — no joke, it’s called the “waggle dance.” Also, honeybees have to visit about 2 million flowers to make just one pound of honey. They’re basically tiny, super-efficient machines working nonstop for the hive’s survival. Without honeybees, a huge chunk of our food supply would collapse.
